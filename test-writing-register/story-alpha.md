# The Bridge Builder

A young architect was commissioned to build a bridge over a rushing river. She designed an elegant stone arch, but the villagers insisted it would collapse. They pointed to every flood in memory. She studied the river's patterns for months, then adjusted her design with deep foundations and reinforcement. When the bridge survived its first flood, the villagers named it after her.

Years later, a great storm tested every structure in the valley. The bridge stood firm while others fell.

**Moral:** The best designs account for forces we hope never arrive.
